Stroke

Overview

  • sudden loss of brain function due to interrupted blood flow causing neuro dysfunction
  • brain cells experience hypoxia (unlike TIA where it is temporary)
  • can cause lifelong disability or death

Causes

  • Ischemic - sudden loss of circulation; embolism or plaques block brain arteries (carioembolic = embolism from heart)
  • Hemorrhagic - blood vessel in brain ruptures (Cerebral Aneurysms) causing increased pressure, increased ICP = decreased brain blood flow => cerebral ischemia

Risk Factors

  • HTN - most common; increased pressure can cause vessels to rupture leading to hemorrhagic stroke
  • diabetes - most common
  • severe OSA
  • heart valve disease
  • MI
  • Aneurysm
  • smoking, vaping
  • sedentary
  • oral contraceptives, coagulopathies, migraines, trauma, drug use

Manifestations

  • Right Hemisphere Stroke - left side paralysis and sensory loss, right visual field loss, aphasia (look straight, but move opposite)
  • Left Hemisphere Stroke - r side paralysis and sensory loss, L visual field loss
  • BE FAST
  • B - loss of balance, dizziness
  • E - vision loss in one or both eyes
  • F - facial drooping, uneven symmetry (Hemiplegia)
  • A - arms weak, numbness
  • S - slurred speech
  • T - call 911 immediately
  • Hemorrhagic stroke: headache (classic tell-tale sign of hemorrhagic vs ischemic), N/V, seizures

Other Effects:

  • Aphasia {Expressive = you say words you don't intend to say, Receptive = cannot understand words}
  • Hemiplegia - paralysis or weakness on one side of body
  • Hemiparesis - paralysis of one side of body
  • Hemianopsia - loss of vision in half of visual field
  • Ataxia - poor muscle control causing clumsy movements
  • Dysarthria - difficulty speaking
  • Dysphagia - difficulty swallowing
  • Increased ICP

Complications

  • falls
  • depression
  • UTI
  • pneumonia

Diagnostic

  • r/o glucose or e- imbalance! (similar manifestations with stroke/TIA)
  • CBC, Coags => r/o hypercoagulability

Treatment

Ischemic

  • Fibrinolysis & aspirin - if embolic (alteplase) => monitor for bleeding, angioedema
  • Respiratory - supplemental O2; intubation d/t to increased ICP and compression of brainstem
  • Antiepileptics
  • Physical Therapy - mobility, strength
  • Occupational Therapy - ADLs, regain independence
  • Speech Therapy - swallowing

Hemorrhagic

  • thrombectomy - blood clot (thrombus) is removed from a blood vessel.
  • decompressive hemicraniectomy (partial removal of skull to relieve ICP)
  • reversal agent for anticoagulants (if applicable)
  • Osmotic Diuretics d/t increased ICP
  • Respiratory - supplemental O2; intubation d/t to increased ICP and compression of brainstem
  • Antiepileptics
  • Physical Therapy - mobility, strength
  • Occupational Therapy - ADLs, regain independence
  • Speech Therapy - swallowing

Nursing Considerations

  • evaluate swallowing ability for choking or aspiration risk
  • falls risk
  • bleeding precautions in patients receiving thrombolytics or Anticoagulants, Antiplatelets
