Involuntary Holds

  • 5150 - 72 hours, criteriaL DTS (danger to self), DTO (danger to others), GD (Grave Disability - kinda like failure to thrive)
  • 5250 - 14 day hold
    • first opportunity for patient to prove that they do not need to be there
    • court will see evidence for and against the hold
  • 5350 - 30 day hold
    • only for grave disability
    • mood stabilizers and antidepressants take 2-3 weeks to work
    • usually doctors may pursue conservatorship

Conservatorship - temporary vs permanent

Riese - court hearing to determine if a person has the ability to take their medication

  • AI definition: A Riese hold is actually a Capacity Hearing, named after the court case Riese v. St. Mary's Hospital (1987), that determines if a patient on a mental health hold in California has the legal capacity to refuse psychiatric medication. If the court finds the patient lacks capacity, the facility can administer medication that the patient would otherwise refuse. This hearing ensures involuntary patients can refuse treatment if they have the mental capacity to make informed decisions about it.

ACE Scoring - Adverse Childhood Experiences

Category Question (Yes = 1 point)
Emotional Abuse Did a parent or adult in your household often swear at you, insult you, put you down, or humiliate you, or act in a way that made you feel afraid you might be physically hurt?
Physical Abuse Did a parent or adult in your household often push, grab, slap, or throw something at you, or ever hit you so hard that you had marks or were injured?
Sexual Abuse Did an adult or person at least 5 years older than you ever touch or fondle you in a sexual way, or attempt/actually have oral, anal, or vaginal intercourse with you?
Emotional Neglect Did you often feel that no one in your family loved you or thought you were important or special, or that your family didn’t look out for each other, feel close, or support each other?
Physical Neglect Did you often feel you didn’t have enough to eat, had to wear dirty clothes, had no one to protect you, or your parents were too drunk or high to take care of you or take you to the doctor if needed?
Mother Treated Violently Was your mother or stepmother often pushed, grabbed, slapped, had something thrown at her, or sometimes kicked, bitten, hit with a fist, or threatened with a weapon?
Household Substance Abuse Did you live with anyone who was a problem drinker or alcoholic, or who used street drugs?
Household Mental Illness Was a household member depressed, mentally ill, or did a household member attempt suicide?
Parental Separation/Divorce Were your parents ever separated or divorced?
Household Incarceration Did a household member go to prison?

Total possible score: 0-10

  • Mood Disorder - inability to cope with emotions

Psychotic Disorders

  • Schizophrenia
  • Antipsychotics - aka Dopamine Receptor 2 Antagonist; block dopamine receptors
    • side effects: EPS, tardive dyskinesia 1st gen, weight gain 2nd gen
    • Common drugs: haldol, zyprexia, risperdal, thorazine

Mood Disorders

  • MMD - major depressive Disorder

    • two blue weeks

    • DIGFAST - distractibility, irritability, grandiosity, flight od ideas, activity inc, sleep dec, talkativity inc
