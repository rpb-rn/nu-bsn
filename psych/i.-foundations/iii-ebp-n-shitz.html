Prevention Strategies

MLP = Medical Legal Partnership

  • interprofessional model working to reduce # of patients with MI related to SDOH
  • tenet: training, the merging of healthcare and legal services for this cause, and advocacy at the federal and state levels

SMI - Serious Mental Illness => defined by the degree of dysfunction it may in ADLs

  • lower life expectancy
  • usually accompanied by medical comorbidities
  • lower QOL
  • goal: improve QOL, manage medical comorbidities

Professional Stigma - care provided to SMI patients is lesser quality d/t stigma

  • Goal: attend educational sessions, know SMI life experiences, develop relationship

Daily Living Issues => Housing, Unemployment

  • inability to get/maintain job
  • Approaches:
    • Housing First - focus on getting stable living first before getting job
    • Treatment First - establish job first prior to getting housing
  • Caveat - getting a job means getting disenrolled from medicaid, job makes it difficult to get treatment

Supported Housing - community-based program that provides housing and community-based treatment and support services to assist clients to integrate into the community and live independently

Social Ecological Model (SE) - Individual, Interpersonal, Organizational, Community and Policy/System

Harm Reduction Model - recovery as a process and an outcome incorporating abstinence in its treatment for clients who have substance use disorder

Common Treatment Issues:

  • Medication Adherence
    • Solutions: Mobile Assessment and Treatment for Schizo (MATS) - mobile phone, reminder meds, FOCUS
  • Relapse - use phone apps too to manage

Community Programs:

  • psychotherapy
  • medications
  • assertive community treatment (ACT)
    • involves an interprofessional team of health care workers (nurses, psychiatrists, social workers, and case managers) supporting a client
    • appropriate for VERY serious patients
  • supported employment
  • supported housing

Trauma-Informed Care

Adverse Childhood Experiences

  • traumatic events
  • essential to unlocking sources of a client’s condition and disorders later in life
  • correlation between high risk behavior

Trauma-informed care - approach that allows the client to feel safe because the nurse is actively listening, accepting, and operating within a framework of compassion

Holistic Nursing and Therapeutic Use of Self

Holistic Nursing - provides the nurse the opportunity to care for the client as a whole

Common Issues to deal with Holistic nursing:

  • dental health
  • eye health
  • sexual/reproductive health
  • cardiovascular health
  • higher rates of tobacco use
  • substance use
  • obesity

Working Alliance - relationship between client and provider or nurse, or other member of the health care team who is foundational to the effectiveness of the client’s care.

Recovery Model vs Rehabilitation

  • recovery is becoming more relevant

Recovery Model

  • shifting the focus from the management of symptoms to life goals and individualizing services for individual client needs
  • opposite of medical model: goal is symptom free
  • promotes increased self-esteem and affirms a positive contribution to society
  • understand that patient will not be symptom free
  • recovery does not equal COMPLETE independence

Rehabilitation Model

  • Development of independence: intellectual, spiritual, physical, and psychological
  • similar to recovery as they promote independence
