Developmental Theories

Erikson - "TAGIR"

"TAGIR" = trust, autonomy, guilt, industry, role confusion

  1. Infancy (0-12 months) - Trust vs Mistrust
    • sense of trust with caregiver
    • realize that they are separate beings from caregivers
    • learns to tolerate small frustration and trusts in delayed gratification
  2. Toddler (1-3) - Autonomy Versus Shame and Doubt
    • give up dependence and assert own sense of control
    • self mastery: move from dependence to autonomy
    • favorite response: "no" - negativism is normal
    • quick changes from happy to crying and screaming
  3. Preschooler (3-6) - Initiative vs Guilt
    • likes to please parents
    • begins to plan activities, make up games
    • acts out the roles of other people
    • develops: sexual identity, conscience
    • feels remorse when makes wrong choice or behaves badly
  4. School-age (6-12) - Industry vs Inferiority
    • Developing self-worth and cognitive/social skills
    • Thru involvement in home, school, and community activities
    • satisfaction from achieving success in developing new skills = self worth
    • if expectations are too high => inferiority
    • Needs support and encouragement from important people in life
  5. Adolescent (11-20) - Identity vs Confusion
    1. Early (10-13)
      • focus on bodily changes
      • frequent mood changes
      • strives to master skills w/in peer group
      • define boundaries with parents
      • struggles to separate from parents while still desiring dependence
    2. Middle (14-16)
      • need for acceptance by peer group at highest level
      • interested in attracting opposite gender
      • conflict w/ parents
    3. Late (17-20)
      • more secure with body image; matured sexual identity
      • ability to understand implications of behavior and decisions
      • Process of emancipation from family almost complete
  6. Adulthood (18-65+)
    1. Young Adulthood( 18-40) - Intimacy vs Isolation
    2. Middle Adulthood (40-65) - Generativity vs Stagnation**
    3. Late Adulthood (65+) - Integrity vs Despair
      • Did I live a meaningful life? Do I have enough retirement?
      • Positive: wisdom, acceptance
      • Negative: regret, despair

Piaget

  1. Sensorimotor Stage (Birth – 2 years)
    • Learns through senses and motor actions
    • Develops object permanence (around 8 months)
    • Begins goal-directed behavior
    • Stranger anxiety may develop
  2. Preoperational Stage - Preconceptual phase(2 – 4 years)
    • Egocentric thinking dominates
      • magical thinking - own thoughts are all-powerful
    • Uses symbols, language, and pretend play
    • Lacks concept of conservation
    • Thinking is intuitive, not logical
  3. Preoperational - Intuitive Phase (4-7)
    • able to classify and relate objects
    • Has intuitive thought processes; knows if something is right or wrong, though cannot state why
    • Tolerates others’ differences but does not understand them
    • very curious about facts
    • May begin to question parents’ values
    • Centering - the tendency of young children to focus on only one aspect of a situation or object, while disregarding other relevant features
  4. Concrete Operational Stage (7 – 11 years)
    • Develops logical thinking about concrete events
    • Understands conservation, reversibility, classification, and seriation
    • Less egocentric, more empathetic
    • Difficulty with abstract or hypothetical concepts
  5. Formal Operational Stage (12 years – adulthood)
    1. Early (10-13)
      - Limited abstract thought process
      - Egocentrically thinking
      - Eager to apply limited abstract process to different situations and to peer groups
    2. Middle (14-16)
      - risky behaviors increase
      - more involved with society/politics
    3. Late (17-20)
      - established abstract thinking
      - critical thinking
      - less risky bejaviors
      - develops realistic goals and career plans
Stage Age Range Key Characteristics and Milestones
Sensorimotor 0–2 years Learns through senses and actions; object permanence; cause and effect; stranger anxiety
Preoperational 2–7 years Symbolic play; egocentrism; language development; lacks concept of conservation
Concrete Operational 7–11 years Logical thinking about concrete events; understands conservation, time, and cause/effect
Formal Operational 12+ years Abstract thinking; problem-solving; hypothetical reasoning; deductive logic
