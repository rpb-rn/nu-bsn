i. Infancy

Age Group Age Range Notes
Neonate Birth to 28 days Also called "newborn"; highest risk for morbidity
Infant 1 month to 12 months Rapid growth and development; start of vaccinations
Toddler 1 year to 3 years Autonomy develops; mobility increases
Preschool 3 years to 6 years Imaginative play; beginning of structured learning
School-age 6 years to 12 years Logical thinking; peer relationships grow
Adolescent 11 years to 20 years Puberty begins; emotional and social independence

Physical

  • average weight 3.4 kg (7.5 lbs) - lose 5-50% of bw
  • double weight by 4-5 months
  • triple weight at 1 year old
  • average length: 50cm or 20 in
  • head circ: 35cm or 13.5 in - increased 10 cm from birth to 1 yr
  • Anterior fontanelle - open until 12-18 months
  • Posterior Fontanelle - closes 2-3 months

Respiratory

  • narrower overall
  • larynx is funnel shaped
  • larger tongue
  • less alveoli
  • high risk of respiratory compromise
  • lack of IGA leads to frequent infections

Gross Motor

Develop from head to toe - cephalocaudal

Age Gross Motor Skills
1 month Lifts and turns head to side in prone position; head lag when pulled to sit; rounded back in sitting
2 months Raises head and chest, holds position; head control is improving
3 months Raises head to 45 degrees in prone; slight head lag in pull-to-sit
4 months Lifts head and looks around; rolls from prone to supine; head leads body when pulled to sit
5 months Rolls from supine to prone and back again; sits with back upright when supported - head lag should be about gone
6 months Tripod sits
7 months Sits alone with some use of hands for support
8 months Sits unsupported
9 months Crawls, abdomen off floor
10 months Pulls to stand; cruises
12 months Sits from standing position; walks independently

Fine Motor

Develop proximal to distal (center to peripheral)

Age Fine Motor Skills
1 month Fists mostly clenched; involuntary hand movements
3 months Holds hand in front of face; hands open
4 months Bats at objects
5 months Grasps rattle
6 months Releases object in hand to take another
7 months Transfers object from one hand to the other
8 months Gross pincer grasp (rakes)
9 months Bangs objects together
10 months Fine pincer grasp; puts objects into container and takes them out
11 months Offers objects to others and releases them
12 months Feeds self with cup and spoon; makes simple mark on paper; pokes with index finger

Diet

  • BM until 6 months
  • 8 mos - add texture to food
  • 6-8 mos - use cup
  • fruit juice - unecessary, do not use until 6 months

Sleep

  • need 10-19 hours per day

Colic - inconsolable crying that lasts 3 hours or longer
