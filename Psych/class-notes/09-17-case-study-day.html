  • UDS: + thc, etoh

  • c/c "i want to die"

  • broke up with gf of 2 years
  • feeling off for 2 weeks
  • CSSRS - screening tool for SI, Columbia-Suicide Severity Rating Scale

    • standardized way to communicate with other nurses in regards to care
  • Questions to ask?
  • Things to look out for?
  • Is the patient DTS - danger to self? Yes, but need to ask more questions
  • "what does anxiety and depression mean to you?"
  • give benadryl, instead of haldol
  • takeaway: need to determine trigger

Case Study 2

  • no psych hx
  • UDS: + THC

  • c/c: "i don't need to be here, you're holding me against my will"
  • brought by friend to the ED:
    • acting strange list last night

    • threatened a man because she thought she was being followed
  • If you were writing the hold, what criteria would you use?
    • ans: DTO
  • Other questions to ask?

  • Patient becomes increasingly agitated during assessment
  • yelling, cursing, demanding to leave, destroying property
  • De-escalate? - yes try it
  • Hands on? - yes, because she already destroyed property
  • IMs? intramusculars - yes - faster than PO medz
  • PRN meds:
    • thorazine for agitation
    • Ativan anxiety
    • which would you pick? - none of them, all of them are PO

Case Study 3

  • 25yo male
  • BIB police from trolley station
  • acting erratic, attempting to charge officers and was tazed
  • Day1:
    • receives 3 I'm meds for agitation over the course of 16 hours
  • Day 2:
    • received patient on AM shift
    • received I'm at 0100 but was not effective, been up since noon the previous day
    • on assessment: patient is friendly
    • thought process: tangential (jumping from topic to topic)
    • speech: pressured (pushes the teeth through lips)
    • mood - irritable, labile (low impulse control)
    • affect: blunted => positive or negative of schizophrenia? ans: negative
    • Appearance: disheveled, malodorous
    • Oriented to name only
    • "i want to go bacj to where I died last time"
  • Scheduled meds:
    • 20 ng PO zyprexa QHS => what to think of this dose?
  • PRN:
    • Vistaril 50 mg PO q4h PRN anxiety => insight: there is no med for agitation
    • answer: no
  • Patient did not sleep the whole night
  • appears energetic, walking into toher patients rooms, causing distress to other patients
  • what other options do we have?
  • In the end:
    • patient was put in seclusion
    • given ativan
  • at 1300, patient assualt staff, code called. patient given emergency 5250 (Haldol, Ativan, Benadryl)
    • What changes are expected to see?
      • zyprexa increased to 30mg PO QHS
      • no PRNs added

Case Study 4

  • 45yo male, received on day2
  • cc: suicidal ideations
  • detoxing on alcohol, lost his job, financial burdens, descrines self as alcoholic, drinks 6 pack a day
  • BAC 300, but oriented to name, place, time, situation. Last drink was 0900 yesterday
  • CIWA - Clinical Institute Withdrawal Assessment for Alcohol => maybe every hour
    • 8 below is mild withdrawal, actual score is 4
    • delirium and tremors = deadly
  • patient complaints of: diarrhea, nausea, sensitivity to light, irritability, runny nose, akathisia (uncontrolled urge to move)
    • actual CIWA score is probably higher
  • Meds: ativan 2mg PO TID, vitamin b 1tab PO daily, abilify 10 mg PO daily
  • PRN: ativan 2mg PO q2h, CIWA > 8
  • 2 hrs later: patient is complaining of same discomforts. CIWA is now probably higher
  • Take away: staff is not fond of this patient, but still as a nurse, treat the patient

Case Study - Heroin

  • same demographic as previous
  • use COWS scale
  • patient is asking for sobuxone, nurse gives it but 2 hours later, patient is complaining of runny nose, diarrhea, tachycardia, N/V. What happened?
  • takeaway: must wait for all withdrawal symptoms to manifest before giving suboxone => wait until they have diarrhea
