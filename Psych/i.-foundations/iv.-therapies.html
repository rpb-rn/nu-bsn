iv. Therapies

Psychoanalytic Therapy

  • based on freud
  • help a client to express any repressed emotions, bringing unconscious thoughts to the conscious mind
  • transference - when the client develops feelings toward the therapist or health care worker in a way that mimics a relationship that was significant in their childhood
  • Countertransference - therapist or health care worker unconsciously has feelings toward the client that reminds them of an individual in their past. It can be positive or negative.

Behavioral Therapy

  • type of psychotherapy that focuses on modifying a client’s maladaptive behavior patterns and responses, and instead utilizing adaptative behaviors.
  • Maladaptive Behavior - Behaviors that prevent an individual from coping with new or difficult circumstances, problems, or environments.

Cognitive Therapy

  • address current issues (from the past)
  • remove negative thought process
  • therapist will often identify the thoughts an individual has that enhance or contribute to one’s anxiety

CBT

  • premise: mental health problem is based in part on faulty thinking or unhealthy thoughts, related feelings, and learned patterns of behaviors.
  • learn better ways of coping (new ways of thinking) that will lead to better patterns of living (behaviors).
  • helping the individual recognize the thought patterns that created their current situation and reevaluate them in light of the current situation and apply new behaviors
  • examples of who can benefit: depression, anxiety, eating disorders, tourette's

Individual Therapy

  • aka psychotherapy
  • promote the client’s well-being and assist or eliminate a client’s symptoms so they can continue with daily life

Group Therapy

  • aka psychosocial therapy
  • groups are formed based on a specific diagnosis to best assist group members.
  • members often act as a support network and guide their peers toward ways to improve coping skills and adapt to life changes
  • varies by age: i.e. play therapy for kids

Family Therapy

  • can be traditional or nontraditional
  • can be related to divorce, the death of a family member, communication problems, substance use, interpersonal conflicts, or behavioral problems, potentially among children

Milieu Therapy

  • form of psychotherapy in which the environment is controlled to assist the client in preventing self-destructive or maladaptive behaviors.

Biological Therapy

  • pharmacology
  • brain-stimulating activities: vagus nerve stim, repetitive transcranial magnetic stimulation, magnetic seizure therapy, deep brain sim, ECT, etc

Telehealth & Online Therapy

  • virtual video conference, telephone, or even online chat
  • for those who do not have the ability to leave home, live in areas where therapy is not close, or even those who cannot meet the schedule of most therapists.

Complementary & Alt Therapy

  • yoga, exercise, etc
  • dietics
