v. Continuum of Treatment

Overview

Mental Health

  • state of well being, can realize own abilities, cope with normal stress of life, live fruitfully and all that shit
  • can handle stress of everyday life
  • as opposed to patient with mental illness - no ability to cope

Mental Health Continuum => range of mental well-being, with excellent mental health at one end and poor mental health, or mental illness, at the other end.

  • everyone falls somewhere in the continuum!

5 Zones of Mental Health Continuum => can fluctuate; not goal-oriented - descriptive only

  1. Excelling - peak, top performance; e.g. marriage, promotion
  2. Thriving - can cope with regular stress, no long-term concerns; normal level performance
  3. Surviving - increased stress & disruption, high risk of regressing => must practice coping mechanisms
  4. Struggling - anxiety, depression, poor concentration, struggling with work; need professional help in managing (meds, hospitalization, counseling, therapy)
  5. In Crisis - state of emergency, need immediate health; suicidal, severe anxiety/depression, social isolation, lack of self-care. High risk for SI. Needs inpatient treatment, intensive mental health care.

Self-Check Tool:

  1. Healthy
    • happy and satisfied with life; emotionally balanced, healthy coping, normal mood fluctuations
    • consistent performance, healthy sleep, self-confidence evident, comfortable in relationships
  2. Reacting - (Common and Reversible Stress)
    • acute stress from life events, requires self-care and social support
    • coping difficulty but daily living intact => FORGETFULLNESS
    • irritability, impatience, nervousness, sadness, worry, procrastination forgetfulness, intrusive thoughts
    • sleep problems, social withdrawal
  3. Injured (Significant Functional Impairment)
    • requires professional care => prolonged distress, poor coping, impaired ADL
    • Anger, anxiety, prolonged sadness, hopelessness, worthlessness, decreased performance
  4. Ill (Clinical Disorder, Severe Impairment)
    • emergency! needs professional treatment
    • inability to cope with stress, extreme thot and behavior changes
    • severe anxiety, panic attacks, deep depression, fatigue, disturbed reality
    • suicidal ideation

Types of Mental Health Care Settings

Types of Mental Health Care Settings:

  • Inpatient → severe dysfunction, safety risk, intense care, 24/7 supervision
  • Outpatient/Community-Based → nonacute needs, med management, therapy, support

Community-Based Care & Treatment => for nonacute needs

  • Primary Care Provider → first contact, monitors overall health, prescribes/monitors meds, refers to therapy
  • Outpatient Mental Health Clinic → psychiatrist, psychologist, APRNs, therapists, med management, ongoing care
  • Case Management → nurse/social worker, coordinates meds, appointments, transport, collaborates with team
  • Home-Based Services → nurse/social worker visits, med support, monitoring; includes ACT (Assertive Community Treatment) model (inpatient-level care at home, improves quality of life)
  • Telehealth / Telepsychiatry → virtual visits, med management, effective as in-person
  • Mobile Crisis Teams → emergency care onsite at client’s location
  • Day Treatment → outpatient center, mental health + job training, nonresidential
  • Family Services → caregiver tools and support
  • Peer Services → support from trained peers with lived experience

Inpatient Mental Health Services

  • Crisis Stabilization Unit → short-term, emergent monitoring, few hours–days (Prof says 24 units max)
  • Community-Based Hospital Facility → psych unit in hospital, short-term, days–weeks
  • State Hospital Facility → serious illness, court-ordered, months–years, often criminal justice involvement
  • Transitional/Respite Unit → post-hospital, prevents readmission, short-term 2–4 weeks
  • Long-Term Care Facility → chronic illness, ADL support, often indefinite stay
  • Substance Use Disorder Treatment Centers → detox, acute residential, MAT (methadone, buprenorphine, naltrexone), psychosocial + peer support

Specialty Care & Treatment

  • Military Veteran Care → VA programs, crisis care, PTSD, MST
  • Pediatric/Adolescent Care → child/teen focused, inpatient or specialty facilities, PMHS trained providers
  • Geriatric Care → older adult needs, long-term programs, government resources
  • Forensic Care → clients with criminal convictions, rehab + safety balance, diversion programs prevent incarceration, court-ordered care

Public Health Model

  • MH is a public health issue
  • Focus of PH Model:
    • promoting mental health well-being
    • preventing mental disorders
    • improving access to community mental health services
    • providing support throughout the disease process
    • supporting recovery,
    • lowering the incidences of death, disease, and disability among those with mental illnesses

2 Categories of mental illness:

  • AMI - any mental illness
  • SMI - serious mental illness - severe functional impairment, resulting in possible permanent disability.

National Mental Health Act of 1946 - more resources into research, mental illness; education/research programs

Community Mental Health Act (CMHA) - called for comprehensive community health centers to offer services to people with mental illness who were deinstitutionalized.

Underserved populations at Risk for M.I.:

  • densely populated lower-income communities => drugs, violence, crime
  • rural areas - lack of access
  • elderly
  • non white
  • veterans
  • homeless

Causes of At-Risk status:

  • SDOH
  • ACE - adverse childhood events
  • Geographical settings - rural areas, poor
  • marginalization - racism and shit

Incarcerated - high rates of MI

1963 Community Mental Health Act unintentionally worsened houselessness by releasing individuals without ensuring housing

Levels of Prevention:

  1. Primary prevention aims to prevent mental illness through client and environmental support, education, and stressor reduction.
  2. Secondary prevention minimizes early symptoms through screenings, early intervention, and managing minor issues like anxiety.
  3. Tertiary prevention reduces long-term disability, focusing on treatment, management, and helping clients function in society.

MTSS - Multitiered System of Support

  1. promotion - universal care for everyone
  2. prevention - targeted
  3. individualized - intensive

Disparities in Mental Health Care

Barriers to Mental Health Care:

  • Lack of available treatment options or providers
  • Inadequate transportation, childcare, or unable to take time off work.
  • Held belief that mental health treatment “does not work”
  • High level of mental health stigma in cultural or ethnic group
  • Racism, bias, and discrimination in treatment settings
  • Language barriers or lack of providers who speak languages other than English
  • Lack of adequate health insurance coverage

Sub-Headers:

  • accessibility - rural areas
  • lack of culturally competent care
  • affordability
  • availability of care and treatment - not enough providers

How to reduce disparities:

  • Provide culturally sensitive client-centered care
  • Increase workforce diversity
  • Improve awareness of disparities related to mental health care and treatment
  • Promote cultural and language competence
  • Provide education during nursing school on the Social Determinates of Health and Mental Health
  • Advocate for system and legislative changes to reduce disparities
