TCAs

Drug Class

  • Tricyclic Antidepressants
  • First generation antidepressants
  • Examples include amitriptyline & imipramine (Amy trips = sedative effect)
  • others: nortriptyline, clomipramine, desipramine doxepin

Mechanism of Action

  • Inhibit reuptake of norepinephrine and serotonin
  • Increase synaptic concentration of monoamines
  • Block muscarinic histamine and alpha adrenergic receptors causing side effects

Therapeutic Use

  • Major depressive disorder
  • Anxiety disorders including panic disorder and OCD
  • Chronic neuropathic pain and fibromyalgia
  • Migraine prophylaxis
  • Insomnia in low doses

Side Effects

  • Anticholinergic effects dry mouth blurred vision constipation urinary retention
  • Sedation and drowsiness
  • Weight gain
  • Orthostatic hypotension
  • Sexual dysfunction

Adverse Effects

  • Cardiac conduction abnormalities, prolonged QT interval arrhythmias
  • CNS toxicity confusion hallucinations seizures in overdose
  • High lethality in overdose due to arrhythmias and seizures
  • Serotonin syndrome when combined with serotonergic agents

Contraindications

  • Recent myocardial infarction or heart block
  • History of seizures
  • Concurrent use with MAOIs
  • Severe liver disease
  • Hypersensitivity to TCAs

Nursing Considerations

  • Key: wear sunglasses, chew gum, use fiber for constipation
  • Obtain baseline ECG especially in older adults
  • Monitor for orthostatic hypotension and fall risk
  • Assess for suicidal ideation especially at therapy initiation
  • Educate on slow onset of therapeutic effect 2 to 4 weeks
  • Taper gradually to avoid withdrawal symptoms
  • Use caution in elderly due to strong anticholinergic burden

Drug Interactions

  • MAOIs increase risk of hypertensive crisis or serotonin syndrome
  • Alcohol antihistamines opioids cause additive CNS depression
  • SSRIs can increase TCA levels via CYP inhibition
  • Anticholinergic drugs increase risk of toxicity

Important Patient Education

  • Report palpitations dizziness fainting or chest pain immediately
  • Avoid alcohol and other sedatives
  • Change positions slowly to reduce dizziness
  • Take medication consistently do not stop suddenly
  • Overdose can be fatal keep out of reach of children
  • Therapeutic effects may take several weeks
