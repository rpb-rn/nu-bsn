SSRIs

Drug Class

  • Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors -> keeping more around, serotonin is good!
  • First line Antidepressants, PTSD
  • Examples include fluoxetine, sertraline, paroxetine ,citalopram, escitalopram fluvoxamine
  • takes 2-4 weeks to reach full effect

Mechanism of Action

  • Inhibit reuptake of serotonin at presynaptic neuron
  • Increase serotonin levels in synaptic cleft
  • Minimal effect on norepinephrine or dopamine

Therapeutic Use

  • Major depressive disorder
  • Anxiety disorders including generalized anxiety disorder panic disorder and social anxiety
  • Obsessive compulsive disorder
  • Post traumatic stress disorder
  • Premenstrual dysphoric disorder
  • Bulimia nervosa with fluoxetine

Side Effects

  • Nausea diarrhea or gastrointestinal upset
  • Headache
  • Insomnia or somnolence => it is not a sedative
  • Sexual dysfunction decreased libido anorgasmia
  • Weight changes gain or loss depending on drug
  • Tremor

Adverse Effects

  • Increased risk of suicidal ideation especially in adolescents
  • Serotonin syndrome when combined with serotonergic agents
    • aka serotonin overdose
    • never mix with St Johns wort, MAOIs, tramadol
    • SRI = Sweaty hot fever, Rigid muscles, restlessness, agitation, Increased HR
  • Hyponatremia due to SIADH especially in elderly
  • QT prolongation with citalopram at high doses
  • Withdrawal symptoms if abruptly discontinued dizziness irritability flu like symptoms

Contraindications

  • Concurrent use with MAOIs or within 14 days washout
  • Known hypersensitivity
  • Caution in seizure disorders
  • Caution with prolonged QT interval especially with citalopram

Nursing Considerations

  • Monitor mood and suicidal thoughts especially early in treatment
  • Assess for therapeutic effect after 2 to 4 weeks
  • Monitor sodium levels in elderly or those on diuretics
  • Avoid abrupt discontinuation taper gradually
  • Watch for signs of serotonin syndrome such as agitation hyperreflexia diaphoresis fever
  • Educate that full therapeutic benefit may take 4 to 6 weeks
  • DO NOT TAKE with St. John's Wort (see Herbal Remedies)

Drug Interactions

  • MAOIs and SNRIs increase risk of serotonin syndrome
  • Warfarin NSAIDs and aspirin increase bleeding risk
  • CYP450 interactions fluoxetine and paroxetine inhibit metabolism of other drugs
  • Triptans increase risk of serotonin syndrome

Important Patient Education

  • Take at same time daily
  • May take weeks for full effect
  • Do not stop abruptly taper required
  • Report suicidal thoughts severe agitation or signs of serotonin syndrome
  • Avoid alcohol or sedating drugs
  • Inform provider of all medications and supplements
