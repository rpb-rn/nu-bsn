Ch.12 - Nursing Management in Pregnancy

Preconception & Interconception

  • should occur any time a health care provider sees a woman of reproductive age

avoid live vaccines: MMR, Varicella, etc
also avoid: HPV

Risk Factors in Adverse Pregnancies:

  1. Isotretinoins (accutane) - birth defects, cleft palate, congenital heart defects, hearing loss, microcephaly
  2. alcohol - FAS
  3. Antiepileptic drugs - valproic acid => lower dose
  4. diabetes
  5. Folic Acid Deficiency - must have at least 400-800 mcg of folic acid daily to reduce neural tube defects by 2/3
  6. Hepatitis B
  7. HIV-AIDS
  8. Rubella - must vaccinate
  9. Obesity - maternal obesity associated with neural tube deffects, pre-term delivery, diabetes, cesarean, HTN, thromboembolic disease
  10. STI - chlamydia, neisseria gonorrhoeae - ectopic pregnancy, infertility
  11. smoking - low BW, pre-term birth

Prenatal Visits

  • delayed prenatal care => associated with increased rates of NICU admissions
  • 2 approach: individual and centering (group or cohort moms)
  • Gestational diabetes => maintain A1cc lower than 6.5, fasting BSL of 95mg/dL or lower

Health History

  • determine LMP to get EDD
  • History: medical & surgical; smoking, alcohol drugs
    • Reproductive History = GP or GTPAL system
    • Estimating Expected Date of birth or delivery: EDB/EDD => Naegel's Rule
    • birth wheel
    • ultrasound = more accurate to determine GA and date the pregnancy

Physical Exam

  • empty bladder first => collect urine while at it (clean catch)
  • soft systolic murmur = may be normal
  • Estrogen => relaxation of ligaments of joints and ribs = larger chest diameter = increase in RR
  • Linea Nigra - runs thru umbilicus
  • Palpate fundus
  • DVT, edema, pulses, varicose veins
  • Cervix Expected findings
    • Gooddell sign - sofetened
    • Hegar Sign - uterine isthmus softened
    • Chadwick's sign = bluish discoloration of cevix and vaginal mucosa
  • Papanicolaou (Pap) Smear - screen for gonorrhea and chlamydia

More assessments:

  • assess fundal height
  • fetal heart rate
  • BP, weight
  • urinalysis: glucose, protein
  • Pelvic shape:
    • gynecoid
    • android
    • anthropoid
    • platypelloid
  • Pelvic measurements:
    • diagonal conjugate
    • true (obstetric) conjugate
    • ischial tuberosity

Rhogam - used to prevend development of antibodies to Rh-positice cells, such as during amniocentesis or spontaneous abortion

Fundal Height = weeks of pregnancy in terms of cm
McDonald Method - lying down, top of pubic bone to top of fundus => knees flexed
=> if growth curve flattens or stable - indicates FGR (fetal growth restriction)

Fetal Movements - lack of these can mean asphyxia and FGR
Education: count fetal movemnts, and it's significance

Contractions

  • Early contraction => lead to preterm birth
    • not to be confused with Braxton Hicks contractions => stop with rest or sleep and are usually felt in the abdomen, not the lower back
  • Signs of preterm labor: contractions every 10 minutes or more frequently, vaginal discharge changes, pelvic pressure, cramps, low backache, and diarrhea.
  • Early detection is crucial, as medications can sometimes stop labor if cervical dilation hasn't progressed.
  • If symptoms arise =>lie on her left side, drink 2–3 glasses of water, and monitor for 1 hour—if symptoms persist or worsen, she should contact her provider.

Obstetric History

GP System
Gravida - pregnancy numbers
Para - Number of pregnancies delivered after 20 weeks

GTPAL System - more details

  • G: Gravida – Total number of pregnancies, including the current pregnancy if applicable.
  • T: Term births – Number of full-term births (deliveries at 37 weeks or later).
  • P: Preterm births – Number of preterm births (deliveries between 20 and 36 weeks).
  • A: Abortions – Number of pregnancies that ended in miscarriage or elective abortion before 20 weeks.
  • L: Living children – Number of living children the woman has.

Example of GTPAL Notation:

  • G3T1P1A1L2
    • G3: The woman has been pregnant 3 times (includes current pregnancy if applicable).
    • T1: She has had 1 full-term delivery (after 37 weeks).
    • P1: She has had 1 preterm delivery (between 20 and 36 weeks).
    • A1: She has had 1 miscarriage or elective abortion before 20 weeks.
    • L2: She has 2 living children

Fetal Assessments

  • Ultrasonography
  • Doppler flow studies - use of sound waves to examine the flow of blood in blood vessels
  • Alpha-fetoprotein (AFP) analysis = elevated => neural tube defects
  • Marker screening tests - aneuploidy (trisomies 13, 18, and 21) and neural tube defects
  • Nuchal translucency screening
    • usually done in 11-14 weeks
    • detects fetal chromosomal and structural abnormalities
  • Amniocentesis - transabdominal puncture of the amniotic sac to obtain a sample of amniotic fluid
    • empty bladder first to avoid risk of bladder puncture
    • fetal monitoring strip is obtained to evaluate fetal well being and baseline
  • Chorionic villus sampling (CVS). - 18g thru suction cathere thru cervix under US guidance; alternative to amniocentesis; rule out genetic disorders, fewer risks
  • Percutaneous umbilical blood sampling (PUBS)
  • Nonstress test; contraction stress test => most common, provides measurement of uteroplacental function
    • must have empty bladder
    • reactive: at least 2 FHR accelerations from baseline
    • Non reactive: if it's not the above for at least 15 sec within 20 min timeframe
  • Contraction stress test
  • Biophysical profile - real-time ultrasound and NST to allow assessment of various parameters of fetal well-being that are sensitive to hypoxia

  • Avoid highly seasoned or greasy foods

2nd & 3rd Trim Discomforts

  • Ankle Edema - ↑ sodium & cap permeability, circulatory congestion, prolonged standing/sitting, Frequent dorsiflexion
    • Get up and move for at least 5 min Q hr, Supportive hose (used for varicosities too)
  • Hemorrhoids - constipation, pressure, sitz bath, witch hazel pads, ointments
  • Constipation/Flatulence: ↑ progesterone, ↓ fluids, ↓ exercise => tx: ↑ fluids, ↑ fresh fruit/veggies (non-gassy), stool softeners
  • Backache: ↑ lumbar curve, ↑ hormones
    • Proper body mechanics and pelvis-tilt exercise
  • Leg cramps: ↑ nerve pressure, electrolyte imbalances, ↓ circulation
    • Dorsiflexion, dietary changes, heating pad
  • Syncope: Anemia, postural hypotension
  • Keep body temp normal, stand up slowly, check H&H
  • Dyspnea: ↓ vital capacity, anemia
  • Sleep propped on pillows

Red Flags in Prenatal Care

First trimester

  • spotting or bleeding possible miscarriage
  • Painful urination may indicate infection
  • Severe persistent vomiting may indicate hyperemesis gravidarum
  • Fever over 100°F 37.7°C may indicate infection
  • Lower abdominal pain with dizziness and shoulder pain may indicate ruptured ectopic pregnancy

Second trimester

  • regular uterine contractions may indicate preterm labor
  • Calf pain worse with foot flexion may indicate DVT
  • Sudden gush or fluid leakage may indicate prelabor rupture of membranes
  • No fetal movement over 12 hours may indicate fetal distress or demise

Third trimester

  • sudden weight gain or facial swelling may indicate gestational hypertension or preeclampsia
  • Severe upper abdominal pain headache or visual changes may indicate preeclampsia
  • Decreased fetal movement over 24 hours may indicate possible fetal demise
