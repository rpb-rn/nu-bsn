Burns

  • results in release of histamine and prostaglandins => vasodilation, capillary permeability => increased edema

Impact on Health

  • decreased immunity => no skin, exposed tissue, decreased senses
  • increased metabolic demand => high risk for malnutrition; higher caloric needs
  • severe dehydration => organ failure, kidney injury
  • edema
  • deep burns => release myoglobin => rhabdomyolysis (Release of protein and electrolytes with damage to muscle)
  • body image issues
  • decreased mobility d/t edema, dressings => advocate to ambulate

Clinical Presentation

In Terms of Thickness:

  • Superficial - Damage to epidermis only. Appearance similar to a sunburn
  • Superficial Partial Thickness - damage to epidermis and superficial portion of dermis and is usually pink or red and blistering and wet.
  • Deep Partial Thickness - damage to deep in the dermis and wound is usually white or yellow, dry, and nonblanchable.
  • Full Thickness - Damage to epidermis and dermis and subcutaneous tissue and is usually white or black/brown, dry and leathery, and nonblanchable.

In Terms of Degrees:

  • First Degree: Damage to the epidermis, such as sunburn
  • Second degree: Damage to epidermis and dermis
  • Third degree: Damage to epidermis and dermis that will require skin grafting
  • Fourth degree: Same as third degree but extending into fat layer
  • Fifth degree: Same as third degree but extending into muscle layer
  • Sixth degree: Same as third degree but extending to the bone

Labs to Monitor

  • Bleeding => Coags
  • Anemia => CBC
  • WBC
  • Electrolytes => CMP
  • Albumin - low levels => edema; also indicates nutrition status
  • BUN, Urea, Cr => kidney function
  • ABG - respiratory acidosis
  • Liver Enzymes

Treatment


Rule of 9s TBSA:

  • One arm: 9%
  • One leg: 18%
  • Head: 9%
  • Torso Front: 18%
  • Torso Back: 18%
  • Groin: 1%

Emergency treatment

  • cool the burn with cool water => do not use ice
  • ABCs
  • 500 mL/hr of Lactated Ringer, then infuse via Parkland formula => 2-4 mL x kg x TBSA %
  • insert foley (goal OUP of 0.5 mL/kg/hr)
  • OG/NG decompression PRN
  • HOB 30
  • Minor burns => 4c's: cool with tap or saline water, clean with mild soap, cover with ointment or dressing, comfort with OTC pain meds
  • Major burns: debride or excision of nonviable tissue
  • Debride within 24 hours
  • Escharotomy - removes stiff dead skin to allow tissues to expand (important for torso or extremity as burns here can cause compartment syndrome)

Parkland Formula => 2-4 mL x kg x TBSA %

Rehabilitative Treatment

  • Range of Motion exercises
  • skin grafts (autograft = own skin, allograft = cadaver or donor, xenograft = animal)
  • Monitor for infection
  • record daily weights

Pharmacological:

  • Pain: opitates, sedation
  • Benzo for anxiety
  • Abx
  • Topical abx with wound dressings
  • Silver Sulfadiazine
  • Silver Nitrate
  • Apiary Honey

Fluid Replacement

  • goal:
    • 0.5 mL/kg/hr of UOP or 30/mL/hr
    • sys 90 mmHg
    • no AMS
    • HR < 120
  • see parkland formula
  • delivery half in first 8 hours, then the remainder over 16 hours

example:: 4 mL x 70kg x 40% = 11,200 mL
Half in first 8 hours = 5,600 mL or 700 mL/hr
the rest in 16 hours = 5,600 mL or 350 mL/hr
